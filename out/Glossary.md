| German Term | Definition/Context | English Translation | Chinese Translation |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Berufsschicksal** | Central to the text. Not just a career path, but the inescapable "fate" or trajectory of life determined by the external, coercive forces of the industrial order. | Occupational Destiny / Fate | 職業命運 |
| **Auslese** | Weber applies this biological term (selection) to the social and economic process where the "fittest" workers are selected by the machinery of industry. | Selection | 篩選 / 淘汰 |
| **Anpassung** | The reciprocal process to *Auslese*; how the worker's psychophysical nature is molded to fit the demands of the machine. | Adaptation | 適應 |
| **Menschentum** | Weber uses this to denote the specific "qualitative type" of human being produced by the industrial environment, rather than generic "humanity." | Humankind / Human Type | 人性樣態 |
| **Lebensführung** | A key Weberian concept referring to the methodical, conscious management of one's daily existence. In this text, it is examined under the pressure of industrial discipline. | Conduct of Life | 生活經營 |
| **Lebensstil** | Distinct from *Lebensführung* (conduct), this refers more to the external "style" or manner of living (consumption, leisure, culture) outside the factory. | Life Style | 生活風格 |
| **Arbeitseignung** | The specific fit between a worker's psychophysical qualities and the technical demands of a machine or task. | Work Suitability / Aptitude | 工作適性 |
| **Gelerntheit** | A noun formed from the participle *gelernt*. Weber uses it to quantify the degree and type of training (apprenticeship vs. quick instruction) required for a job. | Skilled-ness / Learned-ness | 學成度 |
| **Anlernen** | The process of quick, specific training for a machine task, contrasted with the broad *Lernen* of traditional apprenticeship. Creates the "semi-skilled" (*Angelernte*). | Semi-skilled training / On-the-job training | 在職培訓 / 速成培訓 |
| **Apparat** | Weber uses this to describe the totality of the industrial organization—technical, bureaucratic, and economic—as a machine-like structure that functions independently of the individuals within it. | Apparatus | 機制 / 機械 |
| **Gehäuse** | Used in the conclusion to describe the overwhelming, inescapable structure of modern industrial capitalism. Precursor to the famous "steel-hard casing" (*stahlhartes Gehäuse*) metaphor. | Casing / Housing | 殼 |
| **Psychophysik** | The scientific study of the relation between physical stimuli (work load) and sensations/mental states (fatigue), which Weber seeks to apply to industrial labor. | Psychophysics | 心理物理學 |
| **Anlage** | In the context of the heredity discussion, this refers to the innate, biological predisposition or germ-plasm, contrasted with *Milieu*. | Disposition / Innate Tendency | 素質 / 天賦 |
| **Akkord** | Refers to piece-rate wage systems, which Weber analyzes as a primary mechanism for enforcing intensity of labor. | Piece-rate | 計件 (工資) |
| **Arbeitsfreude** | A term from H. Herkner, referring to the subjective joy or satisfaction derived from work; Weber treats this as a variable to be measured. | Joy in Work | 工作樂趣 |
