**Title:** Methodologische Einleitung für die Erhebung des Vereins für Sozialpolitik über Auslese und Anpassung (Berufswahl und Berufsschicksal) der Arbeiterschaft der geschlossenen Großindustrie [Methodological Introduction for the Survey of the Society for Social Policy on Selection and Adaptation (Occupational Choice and Occupational Destiny) of the Workforce of Closed Large-Scale Industry]

**Author:** Max Weber

[**Preliminary Remarks: Origin and Purpose**] (p. 78)
* [Rejection of practical "social-political" evaluation in favor of objective scientific analysis] (p. 81)
* [The dual inquiry: Effect of industry on the worker vs. limitation of industry by worker qualities] (p. 80)

1. **I. Allgemeiner Charakter der Erhebung** [I. General Character of the Survey] (p. 80)
* [**Economic Neutrality:** The survey is not a moral tribunal but a theoretical investigation] (p. 81-82)
* [**Capital Composition:** Significance of fixed capital, cost calculation, and "standardization" for workforce selection] (p. 84-88)
* [**The Learning Process:** Distinction between *Gelerntheit* (learnedness), *Anlernen* (semi-skilled training), and *Übung* (practice)] (p. 92-93)
* [**Differentiation:** Specialization vs. Uniformity of the workforce] (p. 95)

2. **II. Die naturwissenschaftlichen Probleme der Erhebung** [II. The Scientific Problems of the Survey] (p. 80 / p. 96)
* [**Physiological Aspects:** Muscle economy, rhythm, and mechanization] (p. 97-98)
* [**Psychological Limits:** Skepticism regarding the immediate applicability of experimental psychology (e.g., fatigue curves, *mental tests*) to factory realities] (p. 99-102)
* [**Psychophysics:** *Anregbarkeit* (excitability), *Übungsfestigkeit* (firmness of practice), and the problem of monotony] (p. 104-105)
* [**Heredity vs. Environment:** Methodological caution against premature biological explanations (*Anlage*) vs. tradition and influence of *Milieu* (provenance)] (p. 110-116)

3. **III. Die Methodik der Erhebung** [III. The Methodology of the Survey] (p. 123)
* [**Wage Systems as Data:** Using wage books and calculation data to measure "objective" performance] (p. 127-130)
* [**Selection Mechanisms:** How factories select the "fittest" (via dismissal, piece-rate failure, or union entry)] (p. 133-134)
* [**Two Survey Types:** 1. Intensive individual factory analysis (wage books/personnel files); 2. Extensive trade union statistics] (p. 139-140)
* [**Subjective Attitude:** The worker's "inner binding" to the work and the "psychic" reverberations of the job] (p. 141)
* [**Conduct of Life:** The impact of industrial discipline on the *Lebensstil* (lifestyle) and *Menschentum* (human type)] (p. 143-144)
* [**Cultural Significance:** The industrial "Apparat" (apparatus) as a destiny independent of the economic system (capitalist vs. socialist)] (p. 148)
